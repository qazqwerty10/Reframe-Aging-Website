Why I Started Reframe Aging

I did not start Reframe Aging because I was interested in aging.

I started it because I was interested in why some people continue doing the things they love well into later life while others gradually lose the ability to do them.

Throughout my training and clinical practice, I met adults who remained active, curious, engaged, and independent into their seventies, eighties, and beyond. I also met people who felt their world becoming smaller long before they expected.

The difference was often not age itself.

It was capacity.

The ability to move confidently, recover from setbacks, adapt to challenges, participate in meaningful activities, and remain connected to the people and communities that matter.

Reframe Aging grew from a simple belief: aging is inevitable, but many of the limitations we associate with aging are not. My goal is to help people build and preserve the physical, cognitive, and social reserves that allow them to continue living fully throughout life.

The goal of aging well is not simply to live longer. It is to continue living fully.

That idea continues to guide how I practice, teach, and think about healthy aging.

Beyond the Clinic

I am a lifelong foodie, enthusiastic home cook, and perpetual collector of new experiences. My wife and I moved to Denver in 2023 and continue to explore Colorado one recommendation at a time — whether it is a neighborhood restaurant, a weekend trip, a ski day, or a trail that does not require summiting a 14er.

One of the unexpected gifts of working with aging adults is the opportunity to learn from a lifetime of experiences, stories, and wisdom. Every person has a unique perspective shaped by where they have been, what they have overcome, and what they value most. Those conversations are a constant reminder that aging well is about far more than physical health. It is about continuing to grow, contribute, connect, and find meaning throughout life.

How I Work

My work spans recovery, strength development, balance and vestibular rehabilitation, fall prevention, and healthy aging. Rather than focusing only on today's symptoms, I try to understand what people want to continue doing in the years ahead — and help them build the capacity to get there.

Clinical care is currently provided at Mile High Physical Therapy & Balance Centers in Lowry, Colorado.

Ideas That Influence My Work

The ideas that most influence how I practice sit at the intersection of movement science, healthy aging research, and the broader question of what makes a life go well across the full arc of time. I am interested in the evidence on physical capacity and its relationship to independence, the role of environment and community in supporting thriving, the psychology of resilience and self-efficacy, and what clinical practice looks like when it takes the long view.
