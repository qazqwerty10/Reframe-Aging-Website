About

Tim Nguyen,
PT, DPT, GCS

Doctor of Physical Therapy. Board-Certified Geriatric Clinical Specialist. Founder of Reframe Aging. Practicing in Denver, Colorado.

Tim is a Doctor of Physical Therapy and board-certified Geriatric Clinical Specialist — a credential held by fewer than 1% of licensed physical therapists — practicing in Denver, Colorado. His work spans orthopedic rehabilitation, strength development, balance and vestibular training, fall prevention, and proactive longevity-focused care across the full spectrum from acute recovery to lifelong capability.

He founded Reframe Aging after seeing the same pattern repeatedly: people attributing to aging what was actually the consequence of years without adequate physical reserve. The limitations were real. The cause was often not age itself, but the absence of deliberate intervention early enough to matter.

Clinically, Tim’s approach is grounded in the understanding that exercise is the most powerful tool available for extending both lifespan and healthspan — and that building physical capacity proactively, rather than reactively, changes the trajectory of aging in ways that treatment after the fact cannot replicate. Most of what we fear about getting older is addressable. Most of it starts earlier than we think.

Research & Education

Tim co-authored research on Geriatric Functional Milestones (GFMs) — a clinical framework proposing that routine movement assessment across the lifespan can identify suboptimal patterns before pathological onset, enabling proactive rather than reactive intervention.

Outside of patient care, he mentors physical therapy students, leads community education programs in Denver, and continues advanced training in vestibular rehabilitation, osteoporosis management, and longevity science. He is particularly interested in the responsible application of emerging longevity research to clinical physical therapy practice.

The Clinical Approach

Reframe Aging is not a traditional rehabilitation clinic. It is a practice built around a single organizing question: what physical capacity does this person need to thrive — not just today, but ten and twenty years from now? Every clinical decision flows from that question.

This means Tim works differently from most PTs. Sessions are longer and more deliberate. Assessment goes deeper than the presenting complaint. Goals are oriented toward function over time, not just symptom resolution. The measure of success is not whether you feel better after six weeks — it is whether you are more capable, more resilient, and better positioned for the decades ahead.

For clinicians and students interested in this model: Tim is actively engaged in mentorship and education around geriatric physical therapy, the Capacity Principle, and building clinical practices that address the full continuum of aging. Reach out directly.
