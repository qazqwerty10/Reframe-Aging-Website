The Capacity Principle

The difference between thriving and struggling is rarely age itself.

When capacity exceeds demand, people flourish. When demand exceeds capacity, the slippery slope begins.

Capacity is the physical reserve available to meet life’s demands. Walking through an airport. Getting up from the floor. Carrying groceries, traveling with family, recovering from illness — all require reserve that builds deliberately, or quietly erodes without it.

Most decline attributed to aging is driven by modifiable factors: disuse, deconditioning, and the absence of proactive intervention. What looks like aging is often the consequence of a gap that could have been addressed years earlier.

To pick up a grandchild at 80, you need to be able to lift considerably more today. The margin you build now is the independence you keep later. Reframe Aging exists to help individuals build that reserve before it is needed — not after it has been lost.

The goal is not simply to live longer. It is to maintain the capacity to keep doing what matters most — on your own terms, for as long as possible.

Geriatric Functional Milestones

A meaningful change in any of these in the last six months warrants assessment — not because decline is inevitable, but because early identification changes outcomes.

  • Walk without a cane or walker
  • Rise from a chair without arms
  • Stand on toes
  • Navigate stairs one foot per step
  • Get on and off the floor
  • Pick up an object from the floor
  • Stand on one leg
  • Jump
  • Reach overhead
  • Walk six minutes without stopping
Who I Work With

Built for people who think ahead.

Reframe Aging is not a traditional clinic. It is a practice for people who are not content to wait for a problem before addressing it.

The proactive adult

You are active, functional, and determined to stay that way. You are not injured — you are building. You want a clinician who can assess where you are, identify where you are vulnerable, and help you close that gap before it matters.

The person recovering with purpose

You have been through an injury, surgery, or setback. You want more than rehabilitation back to baseline — you want to come out stronger, more resilient, and better prepared for the decades ahead.

The adult navigating change

You have noticed shifts in your balance, strength, or stamina. You are not sure if what you are experiencing is normal aging or something addressable. You want honest answers and a clear path forward.

The lifelong learner

You think seriously about healthspan, not just lifespan. You follow the longevity research, you ask good questions, and you want a clinical partner who understands the science, communicates it clearly, and applies it to your specific goals and life.

Continuum of Care

Beyond recovery. Toward lifelong capability.

Most clinical care engages with aging reactively — after the fall, after the diagnosis. Reframe Aging supports individuals across the full spectrum. Most practices stop at recovery. This one doesn’t.

Stage 01

Recovery

Restoring function after injury, illness, or surgery — with the goal of returning to and surpassing prior capacity.

Stage 02

Optimization

Building strength, mobility, balance, and confidence beyond baseline. Expanding what is possible, not simply returning to where you were.

Stage 03

Prevention

Proactive assessment and intervention before problems arise. Preparing for challenges not yet encountered.

Stage 04

Longevity

Supporting lifelong participation and independence. Training for the tasks that define a capable life at 80 and beyond.
